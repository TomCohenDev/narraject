Character Building Guide

Point Buy by Power Level

Power levels represent what difficulty tier should be "fairly easy" (~70-80% success) when you optimize your build with Rating + Focus + Traits + Assets.

Level Fairly Easy Points Example Distribution Anchors
1 Easy (1 mark) 4-6 (2,1,1,0) or (1,1,1,1) 0-1 basic
2 Moderate (2 marks) 8-12 (3,3,2,0) or (3,2,2,1) 2-3 basic
3 Challenging (3 marks) 12-16 (4,4,3,1) or (5,3,3,1) 3-4 (1-2 adv)
4 Hard (4 marks) 16-20 (5,5,4,2) or (6,5,4,1) 4-5 (2-3 adv)
5 Very Hard (5 marks) 20-24 (6,6,5,3) or (7,6,5,2) 5+ (2-3 adv, 1 master)
6 Formidable (6 marks) 24-28 (7,7,6,4) or (8,7,6,3) 6+ (3-4 adv, 1-2 master)
7+ Heroic/Legendary 28-32 (8,8,7,5) or (9,8,7,6) Full optimization

Example: Level 2 Character (8 points)

Stats: Might 3, Agility 3, Intellect 2, Persona 0

Anchors: Combat Trained (Adv +2d6), Quick Reflexes (Basic +1d6)

Assets: Pistol (+1d6)

Dodging fire (2 marks = Moderate):

3 (Agility) + 3 (Focus) + 1d6 (Trait) = 7d6

→ 73.7% success (fairly easy as intended)

Design Principles

  • Reserve Capacity: Rating 1-5: (Rating × 2) + 5 | Rating 6-10: (Rating × 3) + 2 (max 32 at Rating 10)
  • Focus Cap: Rating + 2 (max spend per roll). Prevents nova builds, creates pacing
  • Optimization Gap: Rating 5: 18d6 max | Rating 10: 31d6 max (with Focus cap + anchors + gear)
  • Specialization: (8,2,2,0) vs (4,3,3,2) both work at 12 points
  • Aspiration: Lower level characters CAN attempt higher difficulties, just unlikely
  • Progression: Characters advance by increasing ratings AND acquiring better anchors/gear
