# Flaws

Flaws are narrative weaknesses, fears, or physical limitations that mechanically hinder your character.

---

## The Rule of Balance

**You must have 1 Flaw for every 1 Trait you possess.**

This ensures characters have both strengths and weaknesses. If you have 4 Traits, you must have 4 Flaws. If you gain a new Trait, you must also gain a new Flaw.

---

## How Flaws Work

When a Flaw applies to a situation, it removes dice from your pool or introduces a fail-state condition.

### Dice Removal

Most Flaws remove dice from your pool when they apply:

- **Minor Flaw:** -1d6
- **Moderate Flaw:** -2d6
- **Severe Flaw:** -3d6

### Fail-State Conditions

Some Flaws introduce conditions that cause automatic failure or complications:

- **Phobia:** If you're in your phobia situation, you must spend IP or accept a complication to proceed.
- **Compulsion:** You must take certain actions or accept a penalty.
- **Physical Limitation:** Certain actions are impossible or require additional resources.

Work with your GM to determine how your Flaws manifest mechanically.

---

## When Flaws Apply

A Flaw applies when:
- The situation directly triggers your weakness
- Your fear or limitation would realistically hinder you
- The GM determines it's relevant to the action

**Example:** If you have the Flaw "Claustrophobic" and you're trying to squeeze through a narrow passage, the Flaw applies. You might lose -2d6 from your Agility pool for that action.

---

## Earning IP from Flaws

If a Flaw significantly hinders the party or causes a failure, the GM may award you **1 IP** for playing your Flaw honestly.

This rewards roleplay and makes Flaws feel meaningful rather than purely punitive.

---

## Sample Flaws

### Minor Flaws (-1d6)

- **Easily Distracted:** -1d6 on concentration checks
- **Blunt:** -1d6 on social subtlety
- **Hesitant:** -1d6 on initiative or quick decisions
- **Gullible:** -1d6 on detecting deception

### Moderate Flaws (-2d6)

- **Nightmares:** -2d6 when sleep-deprived or in darkness
- **Overconfident:** -2d6 when rushing or taking shortcuts
- **Impatient:** -2d6 on tasks requiring careful, slow work
- **Haunted:** -2d6 in supernatural or traumatic situations

### Severe Flaws (-3d6)

- **Phobia:** -3d6 (or fail-state) when in phobia situation
- **Addiction:** -3d6 (or fail-state) when craving is active
- **Physical Disability:** -3d6 (or impossibility) for certain actions
- **Cursed:** -3d6 (or fail-state) when curse is active

---

## Creating New Flaws

When creating new Flaws:

1. **Make it narrative first.** What is the weakness? Why does it exist?
2. **Determine severity.** How much does it hinder? (Minor/Moderate/Severe)
3. **Define triggers.** When does it apply?
4. **Consider mechanical effect.** Dice removal, fail-state, or both?

Work with your GM to ensure Flaws are meaningful but not debilitating.

---

## Flaws and Progression

As you gain new Traits, you must also gain new Flaws. This maintains the Rule of Balance.
